Workshop 1

Brand Journey Framework

Why this comes first. Most creators start building without knowing where they're going (I did). They post content because they think they should. Six months later they're exhausted, scattered, in the same place — and they quit.

These four questions work backwards from your desired outcome to give you complete clarity on what you need to do today. This is your North Star — come back to it whenever you feel lost.

Question 1 — What is my desired outcome?
What do you actually want to happen? Not “grow my brand.” What’s the real, specific goal in 12 months?
Why does this outcome matter to you?
Question 2 — What do I need to be known for?
In order for that outcome to happen, what must people believe about you? What expertise must you demonstrate?
Question 3 — What do I need to do?
You can’t just say you’re an expert. You have to demonstrate it. What actions do you need to take consistently?
Question 4 — What do I need to learn?
This is your starting point today. What skills or knowledge do you need to develop right now?

Brand Journey Statement

Your Brand Journey Statement is your North Star. It puts everything in one sentence so you always know where you’re going and why.

“I want to [desired outcome] by being known for [known for] through [actions] starting with [learning].”

Synthesize your four answers into this one sentence. Print it. Put it somewhere you’ll see it every day. Shoot towards it every day.

Define Your Avatar

You are no longer speaking to a niche — you are speaking to one exact person, your perfect dream customer. This is how you build true trust and relatability, because you're speaking to one person who feels like they've been talking to you the whole time. If you don't know who that one person is, your content will never land. Speaking to everyone means you're actually speaking to no one.

★ Bonus Tip
If you’re unsure where to start, use two past versions of yourself. Maybe 5 years ago and 2 years ago. Speak from experience and explain:

+ How the old version of you could become the current version faster
+ The struggles and pain points you could have avoided
+ Your future goals and how you plan to get there

Core Mission + Ikigai

Your core mission is your “why.” Ikigai is a Japanese concept meaning “reason for being” — the intersection of what you love, what you’re good at, what the world needs, and what you can be paid for. When your mission aligns with your Ikigai, your brand has purpose people can feel.

The Ikigai Exercise

Answer each quadrant honestly. The more specific your answers, the clearer your Ikigai becomes. Avoid generic answers — go deep.

What do you LOVE doing?
What are you GOOD AT?
What does the WORLD NEED?
What can you be PAID FOR?

Workshop 4

Core Values

Your Avatar tells you who you’re speaking to. Your Core Mission tells you why you’re creating. Your Core Values tell you how you show up.

Core values are the guiding principles that shape the way you create, communicate, and connect. They’re the guardrails that keep your brand authentic and aligned, no matter what trends come and go. With them, you build consistency, trust, and integrity in your personal brand.

Ask Yourself These Questions
→ What principles guide my creativity and decision-making?
→ What qualities do I want my brand to always reflect?
→ What do I stand for, even if it’s not popular or trendy?
→ What do I want people to feel when they interact with my content?
→ What traits do I admire most in the creators, brands, or mentors I look up to?
→ What do I refuse to compromise on in my work or lifestyle?
→ When I’m at my best, what values am I living out?

Content Pillars

Your content pillars are the 3–5 core topics you’ll talk about consistently. They give you structure without limiting creativity. Without pillars, you post whatever you feel like that day. With pillars, your audience knows exactly what to expect — and that consistency builds trust.

Each pillar should pass five tests:

  • → You can generate 50+ content ideas from it easily
  • → It serves your avatar's core needs and problems
  • → It connects to your offer — there's a natural bridge
  • → You're genuinely passionate about this topic
  • → It builds your authority in your space

Origin Story

Your origin story is what makes you human. People don’t connect with your success — they connect with your struggle. Your 80% honest story is more powerful than a 100% polished one. Don’t sanitize it.

For each part, be as in-depth as possible. Hit the 10-minute timer, let the thoughts flow, and don’t filter yourself — just write everything down. We’ll whittle it down into the final story after.

Brand Vision

Your vision is where your brand is going — not next month, but in 3 years. It's the picture of the future you're building toward. Without a vision, you're just executing tactics. With one, every decision has direction.

3-Year Vision
Where is your brand in 3 years?
Be specific — revenue, audience size, offers, impact, team, lifestyle.
