Define Your Visual World™

You can watch 10 seconds of a Wes Anderson film and know it's Wes Anderson. You can watch Christopher Nolan and immediately feel the weight of his world — the lighting, the atmosphere, the gravity. Your brand should work the same way. This module is about building that world: a visual identity so consistent and intentional that someone knows it's you before they see your face or read your name.

Workshop 1

Creator Analysis

Before you build your own visual world, you need to develop your eye. Study premium creators in your space and reverse-engineer their visual decisions. You're not copying them — you're training yourself to recognize intentional visual choices.

The goal is to find your gap. After studying premium creators, you'll have a clear picture of what's already being done — and where the opportunity is for you to be different.

Creator Analysis Framework

Enter an Instagram handle and hit Analyze. The AI will pull their profile, feed, and visual breakdown. Add as many creators as you want.

Workshop 2

Creator Mood Board

Your mood board is the visual DNA of your brand. Not a Pinterest board of things you think look cool — a curated collection of images that, when placed together, tell one clear story about the world your brand lives in. The mood board comes before the color palette, before the typography, before any design decisions. All of those decisions should flow from the mood you create here.

Workshop 5

Your Perspective

Your perspective is what makes your visual world yours. Colors and fonts are tools — perspective is the lens. The five elements below build the foundation of how your brand sees, shoots, and shows up. When these are aligned, your content stops feeling like content and starts feeling like a point of view.

The Five Elements of Your Perspective

Element 1
Setting
Where does your brand live? Your setting is the physical and contextual environment of your brand — where your content takes place and what that environment communicates.

Element 5
Visual World Narrative
This is the most important part of this entire module. Take everything you've defined and write a 2–3 paragraph narrative that describes your Visual World. This is not a list — it's a story about the world your brand lives in.
Write in present tense. Write as if your Visual World already exists. Example: "My brand lives in a calm, minimal studio bathed in warm natural light. It feels like a quiet morning — contemplative, intentional, unhurried..."
